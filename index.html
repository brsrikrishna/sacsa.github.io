Our mission

Why we exist

“We founded Students Against Campus Sexual Assault because we felt that there needed to be a direct outlet for students to collaborate with one another in addressing the issues of sexual violence. SACSA is a student-led organization that aims to foster a community for those affected by sexual assault, and we strive to provide a space for healing and productive conversations among survivors and others who are motivated to learn more about this issue.”

— The founders of SACSA

Signature event

Take Back the Night

Every April, we join a nationally recognized college tradition: students march together to raise awareness of sexual assault on college campuses and to stand against domestic violence, trafficking, and gender-based harassment — on campus and off.

It is loud, moving, and open to everyone. Bring a sign, bring a friend, or just bring yourself.
