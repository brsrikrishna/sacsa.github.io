Events

Loud marches, quiet crafts

Some events raise our voices. Others give us space to breathe. Every one of them builds the community that makes change possible.

Every spring · April

Take Back the Night

Take Back the Night is a nationally recognized college tradition: every April, students march together to raise awareness of sexual assault on college campuses, and to stand against domestic violence, trafficking, and gender-based harassment — on and off campus.

At CU Boulder, SACSA organizes our march through the heart of campus, with speakers, signs, and students walking side by side. It is one of the most powerful nights of the year, and everyone is welcome.

Fall semester · New

Stop the Spike

A spikeball tournament with a serious purpose. Near the beginning of the fall semester, we're bringing students together to play — and to talk openly about the drink-spiking and date-rape drugs that circulate around campus, especially early in the year.

Come for the competition, leave knowing how to protect yourself and your friends. Watch our newsletter and this page for the date.

Year-round

Community & craft nights

Not every event needs a megaphone. Throughout the year we hold calm, community-building nights — pumpkin painting in the fall, bouquet making in the spring, movies and crafts in between.

These are spaces to relax, make something with your hands, and talk (or not talk) knowing you're among people who get it. No pressure, no judgement — just community.

Around campus

Tabling & outreach

Look for the bright blue SACSA tablecloth at club fairs, Homecoming, and around campus. Stop by to learn what we do, grab info about support resources, or just say hi.

Tabling is also one of the easiest ways to start volunteering with us — a friendly face at the table makes all the difference.
