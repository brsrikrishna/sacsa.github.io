Resources

Support is here for you

Whatever happened, and whenever it happened — you deserve support, and you get to choose what happens next. These resources can help, confidentially.

If you are in immediate danger, call 911. To leave this site quickly at any time, use the Quick Exit button in the corner, or press Esc twice. Note that pages you visit may still appear in your browser history — consider using private/incognito browsing if that is a concern.

On campus at CU Boulder

Confidential resources will not share your information or trigger a university report. Reporting resources can start a formal process, if and when you choose to.

  • Office of Victim Assistance (OVA)

    Confidential · Free

    Free, confidential counseling, advocacy, and support for students affected by traumatic or disruptive events — including sexual assault. A great first place to talk through options with no obligation.

    303-492-8855 · colorado.edu/ova

  • Counseling and Psychiatric Services (CAPS)

    Confidential · Mental health

    Confidential mental health support for CU Boulder students, including a 24/7 support line staffed by counselors.

    303-492-2277 (24/7) · colorado.edu/counseling

  • Medical Services — Wardenburg Health Center

    Confidential · Medical care

    Confidential medical care for students, including care and options after an assault.

    303-492-5101 · colorado.edu/healthcenter

  • Don't Ignore It

    Information hub

    CU Boulder's central hub for exploring support, reporting options, and ways to help someone else — all in one place.

    colorado.edu/dontignoreit

  • Office of Institutional Equity and Compliance (OIEC)

    Reporting

    Where to formally report sexual assault, harassment, or other misconduct at CU. OIEC investigates and can put supportive measures in place (academic, housing, no-contact).

    303-492-2127 · colorado.edu/oiec

  • CU Police Department (CUPD)

    Reporting · Emergency

    For emergencies, always call 911. CUPD's non-emergency line can help with reports, safety escorts, and questions.

    303-492-6666 (non-emergency) · colorado.edu/police

In the Boulder community

  • MESA — Moving to End Sexual Assault

    Confidential · 24/7 hotline

    Boulder County's community rape crisis center, offering a 24/7 hotline, advocacy, and free counseling for survivors and their loved ones.

    303-443-7300 (24/7) · movingtoendsexualassault.org

National, 24/7

  • National Sexual Assault Hotline (RAINN)

    Confidential · 24/7

    Free, confidential support from trained staff, any time. Phone or online chat.

    1-800-656-4673 (1-800-656-HOPE) · Chat online at hotline.rainn.org

  • National Domestic Violence Hotline

    Confidential · 24/7

    Support for anyone affected by relationship abuse or domestic violence.

    1-800-799-7233 · thehotline.org

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline

    Confidential · 24/7

    If you or someone you know is in emotional distress or crisis, call or text 988 to reach a trained counselor.

    Call or text 988 · 988lifeline.org

Not sure where to start?

The Office of Victim Assistance (303-492-8855) is confidential and free — they will listen and walk through your options with you, at your pace. And SACSA meetings are always a judgement-free place to find community, whether or not you ever share your story.
